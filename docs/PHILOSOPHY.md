# Philosophy

A 5-minute read. Worth it before you start. The honest version of what you're getting into.

---

## Honest truth first

**The first 5 sessions will feel slower than just doing the thing yourself.**

You'll think things like *"why am I telling Claude to remember this — I could just do the thing"* or *"this is a lot of typing for not much."* That's normal.

You're not failing. You're paying tuition.

What you're doing in those early sessions: **teaching the system who you are**. Your context, your voice, the people in your life, the projects in your head. None of that is in any AI's training data. You have to put it there.

After 2-3 weeks of casual use, the system starts to remember enough that conversations get faster. After 6-8 weeks, it starts surfacing things you forgot you knew.

If after 4 weeks it still doesn't feel like it's paying back — talk to your assistant. *"Let's adjust this."* Something's miscalibrated, and you can fix it. But trust the slow ramp first.

---

## Maturation curve

A rough sketch of what to expect:

| | What it feels like | What's actually happening |
|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | Slow, awkward, "why am I doing this" | Onboarding, first captures, getting used to the rhythm |
| **Week 2** | Skeptical. *"Is this worth it?"* | First review checkpoint. Adjust whatever's clunky. |
| **Week 3-4** | First "oh, it remembered that" moments | The system has enough context to start being useful |
| **Week 5-6** | Start using it without thinking | The kernel and `state.md` are doing real work |
| **Week 7+** | Surfaces forgotten things, suggests connections | Compound benefit — captures + context + history |

Don't compare to a tool you tried for 30 minutes. This is a relationship, on the order of weeks.

---

## Single focus (at first)

In your first onboarding, you'll be asked: *"What's the ONE thing you want to use this for?"*

It's tempting to say *"track people AND projects AND learnings AND habits AND..."* — please don't.

**Pick one. Maybe two if they're closely related. Not five.**

Why: a system tuned to one purpose for 4 weeks gets *good*. A system tuned to five purposes for 4 weeks is mediocre at all of them and you give up.

Once one focus feels grooved-in, add a second. The 6-week review is your natural cue.

---

## Organic growth (no big folder trees)

You'll be tempted, especially if you're organized: "Let me set up `projects/`, `people/`, `themes/`, `habits/`, `journal/` upfront, with subfolders for each year, and..."

**Don't.**

Folders should appear when something *arrives* that needs its own space. Until then, everything lives in `inbox/scratch.md`. Boring? A little. Honest? Yes.

The kernel and `context/` and the `inbox/` are enough for the first 4 weeks. After that, **maybe** one or two subfolders will earn their place. Most won't.

---

## The maintenance pact

This is the most important principle. **The system needs you.**

It's not an app you install once and consume forever. It's a relationship. It only sharpens if you:

- **Come back regularly.** A week without opening it is fine. A month and it goes stale.
- **Capture things.** Even small ones. The capture muscle is what makes the rest work.
- **Adjust when something's wrong.** "Let's adjust this" is the most important sentence in your vocabulary here. The kernel is yours — change it.

Your assistant will also ask proactively, every couple of weeks: *"How's the system feeling?"* Answer honestly. Polite answers waste both your time.

---

## You can ask for anything

Don't know if something's possible? **Ask.**

- *"Can I do voice notes?"*
- *"Can I read my email?"*
- *"Can it remind me to call my mom?"*
- *"Can I share something with a friend?"*

Your assistant will research, propose options, and figure out with you whether it's worth setting up. Nothing is automatically off the table.

This is a learned habit. Most people, even with AI tools, ask only things they "know" the AI can do. The most useful skill you can develop here is asking *anyway*.

---

## What it's NOT

To set expectations clearly:

- **It's not a productivity app.** No streaks, no badges, no notifications. It does what you tell it to.
- **It's not a backup system.** If your laptop dies, your workspace dies with it (unless you back it up — that's a later add-on).
- **It's not magical AI.** It's plain text files plus an AI that reads them. The magic is the discipline of capturing and the structure of the kernel.
- **It's not a calendar/email/task manager replacement.** It can connect to those, but it's not those.

What it *is*: a memory and a thinking partner that grows with you, one conversation at a time.

---

## Final note

If after reading this you're still curious — go for it. If you're skeptical — also fine; come back when you have a use case that hurts enough to invest in.

Either way, **don't start because it sounds cool**. Start because there's something specific you keep losing track of, and you want to stop losing track of it.
