# Exercise 2 — Capture a Thought

**Time:** 2 minutes
**Goal:** Build the capture muscle. Notice how it feels.

---

## Try this

Think of something — anything — that you'd want your AI to remember. Some prompts:

- A name and one fact about a person you talked to recently
- A small decision you made this week
- A question you've been turning over in your head
- An observation about how you've been feeling
- A thought you had in the shower yesterday

Tell Claude:

> Remember this: [your thing].

Or, more deliberately:

> /capture [your thing]

Claude will save it to `inbox/scratch.md` with today's date. Take a second to peek at the file (open it in any text editor) — see your own words sitting there.

## Now try the variants

Tell Claude something else. This time, classify it explicitly:

> /capture this is a learning: I work better in 90-minute focus blocks than 25-minute pomodoros.
>
> /capture this is a decision: I'm going to call my parents every Sunday.

Notice how Claude routes them slightly differently in the file (look for the 🌱 marker for learnings, `[decision]` marker for decisions).

## What you'll notice

- Capturing feels almost too easy. That's the point — friction kills the habit.
- After ~20 captures, you start to see yourself in scratch.md. That's when it starts being useful.
- You don't need to capture *everything*. Just things that, in 6 months, you'd kick yourself for not having written down.

## A trap to avoid

Don't try to be tidy. Don't think *"is this important enough to capture?"* — just capture it. Tidying happens later, by you or by Claude. The hard part is the capture itself; everything else is downstream.

## When to come back to this

If you feel the system stagnating after week 2, ask: *am I actually capturing things?* If not, redo this exercise. Capture is the heartbeat.
